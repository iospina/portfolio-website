Overview:

TRACE International was redeveloping their website and wanted input on content strategy and information architecture. TRACE wanted to ensure that its users are having the best possible experience and could easily find all the information they need regarding TRACE's products, services, and initiatives.

Initial User Research

We began our user research by conducting Evaluative Usability Tests of TRACE International’s website. To do so, we tasked five individuals to briefly browse the site and find out more about the company, its mission, the services it provides, and its other initiatives.

Competitive Analysis

We conducted a Modular Layout Comparison, where we broke down the layouts of TRACE's competitors and other comparable businesses' websites into blocks. Doing so allowed us to analyze and identify commonalities in layout, and what worked well (or did not work well) for these websites.

Card Sorting

To identify how users (particularly those familiar with the due-diligence and compliance industry) would naturally grouped TRACE's webpages, we conducted Open Card Sorts. This process involved us writing the title of each of its pages down a their own small cards, and giving the cards to users to see how they would sort them.

Once completed, our team regrouped with our findings, synthesized common categories and came up with a set of categories based on these categories. We then conducted Closed Card Sorts, where we asked users to organize the cards once again, but in the categories we provide them. This process allowed us to validate if our proposed categories made sense to them, and if they required further ideating on.

User Interviews

Through a survey screener, we identified potential interviewees, including those familiar with the due-diligence and compliance industry, but also individuals who have sought membership/subscription services online. We interviewed this individuals in order to gauge their motivations to visit websites that offer such services, as well as to note their behaviors, needs, pains and pleasures when visiting such sites. With this interview data, we performed an Affinity Mapping by writing individual insights on post-it notes and identifying common trends we found throughout our interviews.

Personas & Scenarios

With the trends we identified from the Affinity Mapping, we created Personas, that is, hypothetical users of TRACE's website. Our primary user included Lionel, a prospective member/subscriber of TRACE's service.

Using our persona, Lionel, we created a Scenario, essentially a story providing background and a purpose for him to use TRACE's website, and what he would look for on the site. This allowed us to prioritize the scope of what pages we would have to focus on redesigning for the site.

Design Studio

Through Design Studio, a way of rapidly sketching wireframes, we generated 37 Sketches in just 20 minutes.

Usability Testing

We underwent two rounds of usability testing with 5 users. Testing gave us insight into whether our designs were effective, validating the decisions we made in our designs, but also provided feedback on confusing and ineffective points in our designs. Upon receiving feedback, we iterated on our designs, applying the feedback and testing once again.
